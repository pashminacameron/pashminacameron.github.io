Heterogenous Compute¶

As we reach the end of Moore’s (CPU performance double every 2 years) and Dennard scaling (as transistors get smaller their power density remains constant - so each transistor uses less power), the focus of computer architecture has move towards Heterogenous computing.

A good overview of computer architecture in general and this topic in particular may be found in:

  • Computer Architecture: A Quantative Approach (John Hennesy, David Patterson) Amazon
  • John Hennesy and David Patterson 2017 ACM Turing Award Lecture youtube

What is heterogenous compute?¶

This term is used to cover the mixture of traditional CPU based computing with more special purpose devices that are optimized for particular types of algorithm.

This includes

  • CPUs - It may seem obvious but the thing that makes these approaches heterogenous is that we have a mixture of different compute devices. The highest performance systems make use of the CPU for what it is best at in conjunction with more specialist devices.
  • GPUs - Modern Graphics Processors have features specifically to enable / enhance their use for non graphics tasks.
  • DSPs - A class of general purpose CPU with very different characteristics from a CPU. Suited for stream processing.
  • FGPGAs - Flexible devices that let you implement almost any hardware design you want. This is often the path to a cheap or on demand domain specific accelerator.
  • Domain Specific Architectures (DSAs) - These can still have a high degree of programability but they are hardware that has been designed to take on one class of problems. GPUs started as domain specific processors but now their wide applicabilty puts them in a class of their own.

This section is not intended to be a complete description of any of these large areas, but rather a brief introduction to point people in the right direction.

System on Chip (SoC)¶

Mobile processors and increasingly desktop processors are heterogenous devices. An extreme example is the recently announced NVIDIA Xavier SoC

  • 8 Core CPU
  • 8 SM Volta GPU
  • Deep learning accelerator
  • Programable vision accelerator (vector processing unit)
  • Stereo and optic flow engine
  • Image signal processor
